About me

Cultural Studies graduate. Passionate about Japanese high-arts, literature and cinema (it used to be good Heard about Adachi Masao?). I never read travelling blogs. I don't listen to Podcasts. I bought my first sampler in 2009. You could call it late, but I had to start somewhere. My first contact with the arts happened in 1990, Paris, in the garden of Claude Monet, quickly replaced by literature of Poe, Lovecraft and Murakami Ryu (no, not the Murakami you've read!) Don't ask me why I went to Japan, or what made me learn the language. Escapism, Romanticism and boredom are more realistic answers than loving Sushi or Manga.

Most embarrassing thing I'm willing to admit: I like Kanye West. Well...

Biggest Influences: Ameya Norimizu, Ryû Murakami, Rainald Goetz, Takashi Miike, Hannah Arendt, Keith Matthew Thornton
